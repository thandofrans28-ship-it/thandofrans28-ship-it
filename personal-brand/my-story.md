# My Story (≈200 words)

A reusable personal narrative. Use it for bursary essays, your website's About page, your
LinkedIn About, or as an interview answer. One version at ~200 words, plus a 60-word cut.

---

## The 200-word version

I'm Thando Frans, a Grade 12 student in East London, and I build things.

I didn't grow up planning to be a developer — I grew up loving mathematics, the feeling of a
hard problem finally giving way. Somewhere along the line I realised code was just maths that
*does* something, and I've been building ever since: automation pipelines, digital products,
and Pathfinda — a career path-finding app I designed and shipped to the Google Play Store to
help South African students choose degrees with data instead of guesswork.

I built Pathfinda because I watched people around me make one of the biggest decisions of
their lives almost blind, and I couldn't leave that alone. That's the thread through
everything I do: take something uncertain and high-stakes, and make it clearer.

It's also why I want to be an actuary. Actuarial science is exactly that instinct made into a
profession — turning uncertainty into decisions people can trust. So while I finish matric as
Deputy Head Boy and aim for Dux, I'm not waiting to start. I'm learning the maths, writing the
code, and shipping the products now.

I build while I learn. That's the whole plan.
