Why electrical engineering.

I started with software because it was the fastest way to make an idea real with nothing but a laptop. But the more I built, the more I wanted to understand the layer underneath — the circuits, signals, and power systems that everything I write eventually runs on.

Electrical engineering sits exactly where I want to be: rigorous maths and physics, but pointed at things you can build and measure. South Africa has real, unglamorous engineering problems — energy, reliable infrastructure, access — and I'd rather work on those than around them.

Right now that means pushing Maths and Physical Sciences as hard as I can, shipping projects that prove I can finish things, and heading into an EE degree in 2027 ready to work.
